## Overview of Concept

MindShift is a narrative-driven detective mystery where the player investigates several murders by exploring contradictory perspectives of multiple witnesses, entering their memories to uncover lies and reveal the truth.

Developed in Unity for PC, it delivers a cinematic experience where observation, deduction, and cross-examination are key to identifying the real killer.

Unlike traditional detective games that rely on explicit guidance or objective markers, MindShift emphasizes player-driven deduction. Information is deliberately incomplete and subjective, requiring players to actively compare details, reason logically, and draw their own conclusions. The core mechanic revolves around entering “memory environments,” which are distorted reconstructions of events as remembered by each suspect. These memories may contain omissions, exaggerations, or fabricated elements, especially in the  case of unreliable or guilty witnesses. 

The investigation progresses organically as clues unlock new dialogue options and testimony memories, gradually revealing a clearer picture of what truly happened. Once all relevant evidence has been gathered and all investigative paths explored, the player is given the opportunity to accuse one of the suspects. The game concludes with one of two possible endings - successfully closing the case or failing due to an incorrect accusation - reinforcing the central theme that truth is built through perspective, attention, and interpretation. 

While the full concept of the game envisions multiple cases and environments, each presenting distinct characters and dilemmas, the current prototype focuses on a sorority house murder, serving as the first complete investigation scenario and the foundation for future levels.

### Core Pillars

- **Multiple Perspectives System:** Experience the same event through different witnesses, each presenting their own version of the truth. Contradictions between their stories become the core of the investigation. 
- **Immersive First-Person Exploration:** Move freely through the crime scene, inspect rooms, collect evidence, and uncover hidden clues that reveal relationships, motives and lies. 
- **Player-Driven Deduction:** The game provides tools for investigation but never draws conclusions on behalf of the player. Logical reasoning and observation are essential.
- **Narrative-Driven Gameplay:** A strong focus on story and dialogue. Storytelling is conveyed through environmental details, object placement, lighting, and subtle inconsistencies rather than exposition alone. 
- **No Combat, No Time Pressure:** There are no action-based challenges. The tension emerges from uncertainty and the weight of making the final accusation.

## Project Context

This game was created under the pretext of the subject Game Design and Development from the Computer Engineering Masters, with specialization on Games, Interactive and Graphic Systems, on  Instituto Superior de Engenharia do Porto.

The project was born from the theme "Shifting Perspectives", which is achieved by allowing the player to experience the same events through different points of view, transforming perspective into the core gameplay element.

The assignment time of completion given was one month.

The current version is not the delivered version. Since the delivery of the assignment, the project has received, although minor, several updates to fix any rushed aspects that are common in college projects.

Given the context in which the project was created, and the laws of the institute to created material within subjects, the current game can not evolve, being the current state, its final state.

### Soundtrack
Our goal for the soundtrack was to include the instruments commonly associated with the old detective and mystery movies - the muted trumpets, soft bass, quiet piano, and vibraphone.

The sample that was used in the game is called “Detective Stories” by Francesco Blondi (alias 
Pine/Free Groove, depending on the platform). It meets all the requirements set out, but it was 
slightly altered to match the tempo and the atmosphere.

- Detective Stories, by Francesco Blondi (https://pixabay.com/music/crime-scenedetective-stories-198357)
